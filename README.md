# The Solstice Wheel · *Annus Verus*

**A calendar re-forged from Earth, Sun & cosmos.**

An interactive, single-file web essay proposing a more sky-true calendar than the
Gregorian — distilled from forty timekeeping traditions across five thousand years
(Egyptian, Persian Solar Hijri, Maya, Chinese, Hindu, Celtic, French Republican,
Cotsworth, and more) and the hard numbers of positional and galactic astronomy.

## The design in one breath

- **Born of the longest night.** The year begins on the *observed* December solstice,
  so the seasons can never drift.
- **Thirteen moons of twenty-eight.** Thirteen identical 28-day months = 364 days, so
  every date keeps the same weekday forever; the leftover ~1¼ days are festival days
  that sit *outside* the week, keeping the 7-day cycle unbroken.
- **Eight seasonal stations.** The two solstices, two equinoxes, and four cross-quarter
  days (the Wheel of the Year) are welded permanently in place.
- **An honest Moon.** A parallel lunar track reports the real Moon rather than faking
  lunar months, since the lunar and solar years are incommensurable.
- **Driftless.** Read from the sky, not from a 4/100/400 formula — zero secular drift.

## What's inside

A living orbital wheel you can turn, a plain-language primer, all thirteen re-themed
months, the eight stations, a Gregorian→true-date converter, a filterable library of
the forty ancestor calendars, an astronomical-constants engine room, a sky almanac of
orbit-keyed events, the first stone observatories, and a deep-time finale (precession
of the equinoxes, the ~230-million-year galactic year, and Sagan's Cosmic Calendar).

It is a single self-contained `index.html` — no build step, no dependencies (only two
web fonts loaded from Google Fonts).
